# PCB Slide Rule
## A Slide Rule on a PCB just because I can
<img width="1035" height="843" alt="image(1)" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ab0249b0-6a8f-4d72-9776-9ca331c70f5a" />

Ever had the problem that your slide rule made out of paper was floppy flimsy and not water resistant? 
Now with this PCB slide rule, flimsy no more! It's made out of rigid fiberglass and who knows what!!!

This is a standard KABLCD 100mm by 40mm slide rule (so, quite small) that does

- multiplication
- division
- squares
- square roots
- cube
- cubed roots
- logarithms
- proportions

as all KABLCD rules do.

If you're actually planning to make one:
- sliderule-.zip in /gerber is the final PCB you can upload to a manufacturer
- black solder mask looks best
- the piece on the bottom right corner of the PCB (from the front) is unused and can be tossed out (I'll update this readme with a build guide)

<details>

<details>
  <summary>Q: Can it do addition?</summary>
  <blockquote>A: Repeated addition, yes, this is also known as multiplication. <br/>Addition where the two numbers are different, no</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Q: The spaces between each line on the K scale is so small, will you be able to read from it?</summary>
  <blockquote>A: Most likely not.</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Q: How accurate is the division on a slide rule?</summary>
  <blockquote>A: You'll get about two significant figures on this one. It'll do 9/2 perfectly fine and such but don't expect an accurate result for 16540/323 (yes, it can do that if you try hard enough)</blockquote>blockquote>
</details>
